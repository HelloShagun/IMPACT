Research Overview

The IMPACT group sits at the intersection of satellite remote sensing, structural engineering, and data science. We develop methods that harness rapidly advancing Earth Observation technologies to monitor and predict the health of urban infrastructure at scale — making routine and large-area assessment feasible where traditional inspection is costly or hazardous.

InSAR & Satellite Monitoring

We use Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) from satellites such as Sentinel-1 to detect millimetre-scale displacements in bridges, tunnels, and buildings — enabling structural health monitoring without physical access.

Data Fusion & Digital Twins

We integrate satellite data with in-situ sensors, digital models, and FE simulations to automate anomaly detection and build richer, multi-source structural assessments for built environment assets.

Computer Vision for Earth Observation

Developing semi-supervised and multi-modal deep learning methods to extract actionable information from remote sensing imagery, including land cover mapping, damage assessment, and disaster risk monitoring.

Projects

Satellite Monitoring of UK Bridge Infrastructure

CDBB / EPSRC · Cambridge & University of Leeds

Applying InSAR to continuously monitor bridge assets across Greater London, using custom corner reflectors and automated time-series analysis to flag potential signs of structural degradation early.

MicroBlast: Disaster Risk Assessment from Space

EPSRC · Led by Wanli Ma

Computer vision and minimal-supervision learning approaches to assess structural damage and monitor mass movement risk using satellite and aerial imagery in disaster-affected regions.

Geospatial AI for Water Body Classification

PhD Research · Shagun Garg

Foundation model embeddings from AlphaEarth combined with leave-one-region-out validation to enable spatially transferable water body classification across diverse geographic contexts globally.
